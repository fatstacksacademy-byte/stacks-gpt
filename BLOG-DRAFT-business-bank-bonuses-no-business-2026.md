## Wait — how is a bank bonus "20% APY"?

A bonus is a one-time payout, but you can measure it like a yield. Put $2,000 into a Chase business checking account, hold it ~90 days, collect a $300 bonus, and that's a 15% return in three months — about **53% annualized** on that cash. The deposit never leaves an FDIC-insured account, so there's no market risk to the principal.

No single account pays 20% forever. But by moving the same pool of cash from one bonus to the next — Chase, then Bank of America, then Wells Fargo — your **blended annual return on that capital lands north of 20%**, which is 4–5× a normal high-yield savings account.

> The exact formula: `(bonus ÷ deposit) × (365 ÷ days held)`. It's the same effective-APY math a high-yield account uses — just driven by the bonus instead of an interest rate.

## "But I don't have a business"

You almost certainly qualify already. **You are a sole proprietor the moment you make money on the side** — reselling on eBay or Facebook Marketplace, freelancing, driving for a gig app, tutoring, running a channel. That's a business of one, and you apply with your **own SSN — no LLC, no EIN, no state paperwork** at most banks.

**One hard rule:** never invent a business or fake revenue on an application. That's fraud. You don't need to — legitimate side income already makes you eligible. "You qualify" is true; "make one up" is illegal.

## How to actually run it (without losing a bonus)

1. **Confirm you qualify** — any side income makes you a sole proprietor; apply with your SSN.
2. **Pick the offer with the best return for your cash and your state** — not just the biggest dollar amount.
3. **Fund within the funding window** and hold through the maintenance period (usually 60–90 days).
4. **Track every deadline.** Miss a maintenance date and you forfeit the whole bonus.
5. **When it pays, move the cash to the next one** — that's how the blended 20%+ adds up.

The hard part isn't finding the bonuses — it's the **order**. Your cash can only be in one place at a time, every bank has a different hold and cooldown, and a missed date costs you the entire payout.

## FAQ

**Do I need an LLC to open a business bank account?** No. Most banks let sole proprietors open business checking with just an SSN. An LLC/EIN is optional.

**Is this legal?** Yes — opening a business account as a legitimate sole proprietor and earning sign-up bonuses is standard. Fabricating a business or income is not.

**Are these bonuses taxed?** Bank bonuses are generally reported as interest income (1099-INT/MISC). Set aside a portion.

**Is my money safe?** The deposits sit in FDIC-insured accounts (up to $250k per bank). The bonus is the return; the principal isn't at market risk.

**How often can I do this?** Each bank has a cooldown (often 12–24 months), so you rotate across banks rather than repeating one.
