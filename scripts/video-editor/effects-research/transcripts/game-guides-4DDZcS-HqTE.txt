Now for those of you don't simply want to use my transitions: Let's go back into Fusion and manually implement this nice little transition effect. So the first thing you want to do is press control space, type in transform, add the transform node, and now you first have to think about if you want to create a wipe transition that is moving your first clip to the top, bottom, right, or left. In my case I want to wipe this first clip to the top and then bring in the second clip from the bottom. To do this, go to the beginning of your timeline, click on create keyframe on the position of your clip, go to the end of the timeline, create another keyframe, and make this move up. In that case we have to type in a value of 1.5, in order to fully move the clip out of the screen. Of course this doesn't look very nice so we also have to do something that reappears here and in that case we go to edges and click on mirror. So as you can see that way the clip is nicely moving out of our screen to the top. Now hit control space again, type in a directional blur, now enter an angle of 90 as we wanna generate blur in the vertical direction, go to the beginning of the timeline, hit generate keyframe on length, go to the end of the timeline, and generate another keyframe at length of 0.1. If you want to you could even go a little bit higher here - this all depends on how much blur you like. Next press control space again, type in dent, use the Dent2 type, and use a bit of a bigger size of one. Then go to the beginning of the timeline, add a keyframe of zero, go to the end of the timeline, and add another strength keyframe at 0.6. And finally most importantly go to the settings tab and disable one of the color channels. And what this does is it kind of generates this RGB split effect, which does look really nice! And as you can see we already have a pretty nice wipe roll effect. However, the effect does start quite abrupt here at the beginning. So we're gonna have to edit our splines. For that right click on transform, click on edit splines, now this spline window will open, select the transform node, click on the first little rectangle here, and drag this line pretty much until it's parallel to this second square. Now what this does is it basically makes the animation very smooth. Now disable the transform node, enable the directional blur node, again click on the first rectangle, and now this time we want to make kind of a s-shaped spline like this. And finally for the dent you probably have to zoom in quite a little bit, click on the first square, and just drag this until it's somewhat horizontal.
