## 2. The universal structure (every winner follows this skeleton)

1. **Hook** (authority or open-loop, see above)
2. **"Not sponsored" disclaimer** — *every single one* says it. Daniel Sparks builds a whole bit around
   it ("most companies put a leash on me… people can tell when you read a script"). Magnified Money:
   "this is not sponsored by SoFi." → This authenticity beat is non-negotiable in the genre.
3. **The welcome bonus first** (Spencer: "I start here because this alone might make it worth opening
   even if you don't stay"). For a bonus channel this is exactly right.
4. **Pros / feature deep-dives** — with screen-share demos (see §3).
5. **A hard pivot to CONS** — "now let's talk about the drawbacks." This is the retention payoff and the
   trust-builder. The cons section is *longer and more detailed* than you'd expect (see §4).
6. **Honest alternative** — "if you won't do direct deposit, go look at Marcus/Ally instead." They give
   away the alternative on purpose; it builds trust and keeps the bonus-seekers.
7. **Verdict + CTA** — affiliate link as "support this free content," comment-bait ("what's your favorite
   feature?"), and a link to their "best HYSA" video.

## 4. The CONS checklist — cover every one of these or you look less honest than they do

This is the single biggest lesson. The winners win on the **cons** section. Hit all of these:

| Con | How they cover it | Detail level |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct-deposit requirement** | THE universal catch — every video hammers it; without DD savings drops to ~0.8–1.2% | High |
| **Cash deposits via Green Dot** | $4.95/deposit, $500/txn, daily/weekly caps, "cashiers don't know what it is" | **Highest** — Daniel Sparks field-tested it at 7-Eleven (clueless) then CVS |
| **No physical branches** | online-only; the reason the APY is high (low overhead) | Med |
| **Variable APY** | rates change with the Fed; "depending on when you're watching this" | Med |
| **Account freezes / fraud holds** | Spencer cites Reddit complaints; advises a backup account | Med (the trust-credible one) |
| **Aggressive in-app cross-sell** | constant loan/refi/partner notifications (Daniel + Spencer) | Med |
| **Taxes on interest** | 1099-INT; "you'll owe a bit at tax time" (Feasible Creative) | Low |
| **Buy-now-pay-later in the debit card** | Daniel Sparks' ethical critique — "preys on paycheck-to-paycheck" | Low (differentiator) |
| **Customer-service hours / AI chatbot** | human support limited hours; off-hours = bot (Your Finance Friend) | Low |

## 5. The "secret"/insider beats that bonus audiences reward

- **Stacking the bonus with Rakuten** — Spencer's standout move: open SoFi through Rakuten for an extra
  ~$400 / 40K points *on top* of SoFi's own bonus. This is the insider beat that fits your "secret/hidden
  bonuses" title. **You can go further:** referral $25 stack + Rakuten + the 0.7% APY boost + which
  account to route the DD through.
- **The "qualifying deposits" loophole** — Spencer (firsthand, recent): you keep the elevated APY with
  **$5,000 in regular external transfers every 31 days**, not just employer direct deposit. Huge for the
  self-employed. (Note: the $400 *bonus* still needs true employer DD — keep that distinction clean.)
- **SoFi Plus break-even math** — Spencer: "$8,600 in savings is the break-even on the $120/yr Plus fee
  via the 4.5% tier." Concrete math like this reads as expertise.

## 6. ⚠️ What's now OUTDATED in these videos (don't copy their numbers)

The popular videos are mostly 1–2 years old and quote **stale figures**. Use the corrected numbers from
your fact-check section, not theirs:
- Feasible Creative / Your Finance Friend / Greater Than Enough quote **"4.6% APY," "$50/$250–$300,"
  "$2M FDIC," "1.2% no-DD"** — all stale.
- **Spencer Johnson is the freshest and most accurate**, and he explicitly *corrects* the others:
  **$400 bonus**, **3.1% base APY** (just dropped from 3.3%), **0.8% no-DD**, **4.5% Plus on $20K**,
  **up to 3.8% with the 0.7% boost through end of 2026**, **$3M FDIC**.
- **The SoFi Plus nuance (resolves the earlier flag):** Spencer says **as of March 31, 2026 SoFi Plus
  itself went to $10/month**, BUT **the elevated APY is decoupled** — you still get the high base rate
  (and the 0.7% boost) **free** with direct deposit or $5K qualifying deposits. The **4.5%-on-$20K tier**
  is the part that the $10/mo Plus unlocks. So the precise on-camera framing is:
  > "The high APY is free with direct deposit. The premium 4.5%-on-$20k tier is the paid SoFi Plus perk —
  > $10/month, and it pays for itself above ~$8,600 in savings."
  This conflicts with SoFi's *current marketing page* (still lists DD as a free path to Plus), so this is
  the one thing to confirm live before recording.
