## ⚠️ FACT-CHECK BEFORE YOU RECORD — current numbers (sources conflict; these are live)

**✅ VERIFIED — safe to say:**
1. **Bonus: $50 / $400** (NOT $300). $50 for $1,000–$4,999 in DD; **$400 for $5,000+** within
   **25 days** of first eligible DD. Window **5/15–12/31/2026**. Taxable (1099).
2. **Savings APY: 3.1%** base with DD (recently dropped from 3.3%) · **0.8%** no-DD · **up to 3.8%**
   with the limited-time **0.7% boost** (through end of 2026) · **4.5%** for SoFi Plus on the first
   **$20,000**. Always say **"up to 3.8%,"** never a flat 3.8%.
3. **Card:** 2% flat → **2.2% with direct deposit** (3% travel → 3.3%). **No welcome bonus** (sometimes
   a 3%-first-year promo). $0 AF, no foreign tx fee.
4. **Invest stock grab:** fund **$50 in 45 days** → stock worth **$5–$1,000** (most people get $5–$10 —
   say that). Plus a **1% match on IRA contributions**.
5. **FDIC:** **up to $3 million** via the partner-bank network (recently raised from $2M).

**🚩 THE SoFi PLUS NUANCE (this is the part every old video gets wrong — get it right):**
- As of **March 31, 2026**, the **SoFi Plus *membership* costs $10/month.** BUT the **elevated APY is
  decoupled from it** — you still get the high base rate (3.1%) **and** the 0.7% boost **free** as long
  as you have **direct deposit OR $5,000 in qualifying deposits every 31 days.**
- The **$10/month only buys the premium 4.5%-on-$20k tier** (+ 1% invest match, 10% card boost,
  planners). Break-even on the $120/yr is about **$8,600** in savings.
- On-camera line: *"The high APY is free with direct deposit. The $10 only buys the 4.5% tier."*
- ⚠️ SoFi's current marketing page still lists DD as a free path to *Plus* — this conflicts with the
  March 31 change. **Pull up sofi.com/sofi-plus the morning you film and confirm which is live.**

**🚩 THE QUALIFYING-DEPOSIT LOOPHOLE (great beat, state it carefully):**
- To keep the **elevated APY**, you don't strictly need an employer paycheck — **$5,000 in regular
  external transfers every 31 days** counts as "qualifying deposits." Huge for the self-employed.
- BUT the **$400 sign-up bonus** still requires **true employer/government direct deposit** — transfers
  do NOT count for the bonus. Keep these two things separate so you don't mislead anyone.

**🚩 RAKUTEN STACK (verify the live amount):** opening SoFi *through Rakuten* can pay cash back **on top
  of** SoFi's bonus. The amount **fluctuates a lot** (often $50–$125+, occasionally much higher). Don't
  quote a fixed number — say "check what Rakuten's SoFi offer is the day you sign up."

## PART 3 — THE CONS (⭐ the retention centerpiece — go deep here) (~2.5 min)

> "Alright — this is the part most reviews rush through and it's exactly why you stuck around. Here's
> everything that's actually annoying about SoFi, from someone who'll tell you the truth."

**The cash-deposit nightmare (lead the cons with this — it's the most-watched beat in every top video):**
> "SoFi is online-only, no branches — which is *why* the APY is high, they have no overhead. But it means
> depositing **cash** is genuinely painful. You can't use an ATM for it. You have to go to a Green Dot
> retailer — CVS, Walgreens, 7-Eleven — swipe your debit card at the register, and hand the cashier your
> cash. And there's a catch on the catch: it's up to **$4.95 per deposit**, capped at **$500 a
> transaction**, $1,000 a day, $5,000 a month — and half the cashiers have no idea what Green Dot even is."

> [B-ROLL IDEA — film this, it's what made the 194K video land: actually go try to deposit cash at a
> 7-Eleven and a CVS on camera. The 'employee has no clue' moment is gold.]

> "Withdrawing cash is fine and free at 55,000 Allpoint ATMs — it's only *depositing* cash that's a
> mess. My workaround: keep one free local credit-union account, deposit cash there, transfer it over."

**Account freezes (the trust-credible con):**
> "The one that would actually scare me: search Reddit and you'll find people whose accounts got
> temporarily **frozen** by SoFi's fraud detection on totally legit deposits. It's not common, but it
> happens — so the rule with any online bank is **never keep all your money in one place.** Have a backup."

**The constant cross-sell:**
> "They will *blow up your phone* with notifications pushing loans, refinancing, partner products. You
> can turn most of it off in settings, but out of the box it's a lot."

**Buy-now-pay-later baked into the debit card:**
> "They build buy-now-pay-later right into the debit card. They're not predatory about late fees, but
> BNPL on a *debit card* nudges people to overspend, and I don't love seeing it from a bank that otherwise
> helps you save."

**The honest small stuff:**
> "Round it out: the APY is **variable** — it just dropped from 3.3 to 3.1, it'll keep moving with the
> Fed. You owe **taxes** on the interest and the bonus, you'll get a 1099. Customer service is online-only
> and the human hours are limited — off-hours you're talking to a bot. None of these are dealbreakers for
> me, but you should know them going in."
