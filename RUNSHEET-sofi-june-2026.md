## The accounts
- Quick tour so the bonuses make sense.
- **Checking & Savings** — combo, can't get one without the other. No fees, no minimums, 0.5% on checking, savings is where the 3.1%-up-to-3.8% lives. Vaults = sub-accounts (emergency, travel, taxes), each earns the same APY. Autopilot auto-saves on payday, plus roundups. Vaults are the feature people fall in love with.
- **SoFi Plus** — premium tier, $10/mo since March 31. You do NOT need it for the high APY — that's free with direct deposit. The $10 only buys the 4.5% tier on first $20k, plus 1% invest match and 10% card boost. Above ~$8,600 in savings it pays for itself; below that, skip it and keep the free 3.8%.
- **The card** — flat 2% on everything, 2.2% with direct deposit, no annual fee, no foreign tx fee. No sign-up bonus — keeper card, not a churn card.
- **Invest** — beginner-friendly brokerage with the free stock and 1% IRA match. Not a power-user platform, uninvested cash earns nothing — but clean and the match is real.

## Cons (retention centerpiece)
- This is the part most reviews rush through — exactly why you stuck around. Everything actually annoying about SoFi, from someone who'll just tell you the truth.
- **The cash-deposit nightmare** — online-only, no branches (*why* the APY is high — no overhead). Depositing cash is genuinely painful. Can't use an ATM. Go to a Green Dot retailer (CVS, Walgreens, 7-Eleven), swipe debit at the register, hand the cashier cash. Catch on the catch: up to $4.95 per deposit, capped at $500/transaction, $1,000/day, $5,000/month — and half the cashiers have no idea what Green Dot is.
- [B-ROLL: actually try to deposit cash at a 7-Eleven and a CVS on camera — the "employee has no clue" moment. This is what made the 194K video land.]
- Withdrawing cash is fine and free at 55,000 Allpoint ATMs — it's only *depositing* that's a mess. Workaround: keep one free local credit-union account, deposit cash there, transfer it over.
- **Account freezes** — the one that would actually scare me. Search Reddit — people whose accounts got temporarily frozen by SoFi's fraud detection on totally legit deposits. Not common, but it happens. Rule with any online bank: never keep all your money in one place. Always have a backup.
- **The constant cross-sell** — they'll blow up your phone with notifications pushing loans, refinancing, partner products. Can turn most of it off in settings, but out of the box it's a lot.
- **BNPL baked into the debit card** — buy-now-pay-later built right into the debit card. Not predatory about late fees, but BNPL on a *debit* card nudges people to overspend — don't love seeing that from a bank that otherwise helps you save.
- **The honest small stuff** — APY is variable (just dropped 3.3 → 3.1, will keep moving with the Fed). You owe taxes on interest AND the bonus, you'll get a 1099. Customer service is online-only with limited human hours — off-hours you're talking to a bot. None are dealbreakers for me, but know them going in.
