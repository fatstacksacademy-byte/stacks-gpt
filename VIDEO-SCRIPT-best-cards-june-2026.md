## ⚠️ Fact-check notes before you record (corrections vs. the original framing)

Research turned up a few things that change the talking points — read these first:

1. **USAA Eagle Adapt is CASH BACK, not "3X points," and the cap is QUARTERLY, not monthly.**
   It's **3% cash back on the first $3,000 in COMBINED spend per quarter**, then 1%. Don't say
   "3X points up to a monthly cap" on camera — say "3% back on eight categories, first $3,000 a quarter."
2. **U.S. Bank Cash+ $250 is the best CURRENT offer, but not a literal all-time record** — it's a
   re-match of a level the card hit before. Safe phrasing: "elevated to $250, the best this card offers."
   (Avoid the exact words "all-time high.")
3. **Chase Ink approval rule changed (late 2025): it's now once-per-lifetime, and the two no-fee Ink
   cards (Cash + Unlimited) share ONE bonus** via the "any other no-annual-fee Chase business card"
   language. So you generally get one bonus between them, not one each. Frame the Cash and Unlimited
   as an either/or this month.
4. **CSP got refreshed June 15, 2026 — older articles are wrong now.** The 10% anniversary bonus is
   GONE for new applicants, and the flat 1.25¢ Chase Travel value was replaced by "Points Boost" back
   in Oct 2025. What's NEW: hotel credit doubled to $100, a new $120 Global Entry credit, and a free
   year of Apple TV+.
5. **Bank of America Preferred Rewards was renamed "BofA Rewards" and re-tiered May 27, 2026.** The 75%
   boost now needs **$1M+**; the $100k tier is now 50%. Don't quote the old "Platinum Honors = 5.25%" math.
6. **0% intro APR** — you asked to call it out on #4 and #5 as well as #6. Lengths confirmed: BoA = 15
   billing cycles, U.S. Bank = 15 billing cycles, USAA = 15 months. (CSP has NO 0% intro APR.)
