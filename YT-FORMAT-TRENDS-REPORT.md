# YouTube Format Trends + Where Fat Stacks Should Play (June 2026)

_Generated 2026-06-20. Primary data: YouTube Data API v3 scans (real view counts), last ~2 months. Tooling: `scripts/yt-research/format-radar.ts` (cross-niche format scan), `format-finance-check.ts` (finance saturation), plus existing `_velocity` / `_mine` scans. Web corroboration: vidIQ, OutlierKit, Fourthwall 2026 trend reports._

---

## TL;DR

- The format winning YouTube right now, across **every** category, is **first-person experiential**: *"I tried every…", "I tested…", "I spent / I lived on… for X days", challenges, transformations.* Four variants of it occupy the top of the leaderboard and together captured **~360M views in 2 months** in my scan.
- That exact family is **wide open in the bank-bonus / churning niche.** Best-ever "I tried every bank bonus" video = **12.3K views** (a UK channel). Nobody serious has made it in the US market.
- It also happens to be **the one thing you can do that competitors can't fake**: you actually open the accounts and churn with real money. Your unfair advantage maps onto the #1 format on the platform.
- Tier lists / "I ranked every credit card" / "the only 5 cards you need" are **already won** in finance (Braun 418K, Humphrey Yang 804K, Spencer Johnson). Don't lead with those — you'd be fighting entrenched algorithm momentum.
- Your real bottleneck is still **discovery/packaging** (0 browse traffic, ~1.5K median, everything dies in the sub feed). Experiential formats help because the titles/thumbnails are inherently clickable — but only if the thumbnail discipline comes with them.

6. **Loss-prevention reframes:** "You're Losing $1,400 a Year Leaving Cash in [Big Bank]" — this is the dominant finance CTR format, and it's already your second-biggest video's DNA ("DOUBLE Your Savings APY" = 10K).

**Avoid leading with:** generic "I Ranked Every Credit Card" tier lists, "only 5 cards" listicles, and monthly roundups *as discovery plays* (keep roundups for subs/search — they just won't break out).

## 5. The honest caveat

Format is the lever you asked about, and the lane is real. But your numbers say discovery is the actual ceiling: **0 browse traffic on virtually every video, ~75–80% of views from the sub feed, ~1.5K median.** Competitors beat near-identical videos 60–400×. A great format helps *because* "I Tried Every Bank Bonus" forces a clickable title + thumbnail (your face reacting, a stack of cash, a before/after number) — but if the thumbnail discipline doesn't come with it, even the open lane will stay stuck in the sub feed. Make the experiential video **and** treat the thumbnail/title as half the work.
