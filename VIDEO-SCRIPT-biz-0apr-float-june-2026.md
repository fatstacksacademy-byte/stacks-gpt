## SEGMENT 2 — The 0% float, step by step (~2 min) — the core
> "Normally you pay a card in full every month or the 18–25% interest eats your rewards. But these
> business cards give you 0% for the first 12 billing cycles. So I buy like normal, set autopay to
> just the minimum, and leave my real cash in my high-yield account earning ~4.5%. Amex holds the
> bill; I make the interest. Then I mark my calendar — it's on the bottom of every statement — and pay

HYSA; set ~15% aside for the 1099-INT tax on the interest; some merchants don't take Amex (your health

## SEGMENT 5 — The traps (~60s)
1. **Capital One Spark** — no 0% intro anymore, *and* Cap One business reports to personal credit. The float would wreck your score. Skip for this.
2. **"0%" that's balance-transfer only** (Ent, Blue FCU, RBFCU) — useless for spending float.
3. **Charge cards** (Mercury, Novo) — no APR to float; you pay in full.
4. **Carrying debt / bad budgeting** — if you can't pay before the promo ends, this is a trap that costs you money. Don't.
