# Stacks OS — Product Demo / Walkthrough (Video Script)

Use as: a standalone "here's the free tool I built" demo, OR cut sections into other videos
as the payoff after you've shown a bunch of bonuses manually.
Theme: **"Free does the tracking. Pro does the thinking."** Show the spreadsheet pain, then
show the app doing the sequencing math no spreadsheet can.

Format note: lines in **>** are spoken VO. Lines in **[SCREEN: …]** are the screen-recording
/ B-roll you cut to. Record the screen captures first, then voice over them.

---

## ⚠️ Before you record — keep the demo honest
- Demo on a **real account with real data** (your own profile). Don't fake numbers on screen —
  this audience will notice, and the whole pitch is "the math is trustworthy."
- Confirm the exact **price** and **catalog counts** on screen the day you record (they change):
  the app currently tracks ~**172 checking**, **51 savings**, and **342 credit-card** bonuses.
  Say "hundreds of bonuses" if you don't want to commit to a number that drifts.
- **Free vs Pro:** Free = catalog + tracker + cooldown/deadline math + basic 5/24 + fee-strategy.
  Pro = the three sequencers (paycheck/savings/credit card), 0% APR arbitrage, debt simulator,
  tax summary. Don't promise a Pro feature is free.

> open, when the direct deposit had to hit, when it was safe to close, how it stacked against my
> 5/24 — all of it, by hand. It worked, but it took hours, and one wrong date cost me a bonus.
> So I built the thing I wished existed. It's called Stacks OS, the core of it is free, and in
> the next few minutes I'm going to show you exactly what it does."

[SCREEN: quick montage — messy spreadsheet → cut to the clean Stacks OS dashboard]

## THE PROBLEM (~20s) — make them feel the pain

> "Here's why a spreadsheet falls apart. Every bonus has a direct-deposit minimum, a deadline, a
> hold period, a cooldown before you can do it again, and a fee you might have to dodge. Now try
> to run four bonuses at once across two paychecks without two of them needing the same direct
> deposit in the same week. That's not a tracking problem anymore — that's a sequencing problem.
> And sequencing is exactly what a spreadsheet can't do for you."

[SCREEN: spreadsheet with red overlapping dates, a "❌ both need DD this week" annotation]

[SCREEN: open an active bonus, show the step checklist + a "12 days to deadline" + "safe to close in 4 days"]

## PART 2 — THE FEE-STRATEGY CALCULATOR (free, but show it off) (~25s)

> "Here's a free feature I'm genuinely proud of. A lot of bonus accounts charge a monthly fee
> unless you dodge it. People panic and park thousands of dollars just to waive a five-dollar
> fee — and don't realize that money would've earned more sitting in their high-yield savings.
> So for every fee bonus, Stacks OS does the math four ways: just pay the fee, waive it with a
> direct deposit, waive it by parking a balance, or close early if there's no clawback — and
> tells you the cheapest path in dollars. That one calculation has saved me real money."

[SCREEN: open a fee-bearing bonus, show the four strategies ranked with net-bonus dollar amounts]

## PART 3 — PRO: THE SEQUENCERS (the "thinking" — this is the wow) (~70s)

> "Now the part that made me build this whole thing — the sequencers. This is what the spreadsheet
> could never do. There are three of them."

**Paycheck / checking sequencer (lead with this one):**
> "Number one, the paycheck sequencer. You tell it one thing — your paycheck. How much, how often,
> and how many direct-deposit slots you can run. And it hands you a week-by-week plan: open THIS
> account first, route your direct deposit here, collect the bonus on this date, then move to the
> next one. It's ranked by dollars-per-week, so you're always doing the highest-velocity bonus
> your paycheck can actually support — and it skips the ones your paycheck's too small for and
> tells you why."

[SCREEN: enter a paycheck amount → the sequence re-builds live → show week-by-week slots with payout dates + a "$/week" rank]

> "Watch this — I'll bump the paycheck up, and the entire queue re-ranks, because bigger
> paychecks unlock bigger bonuses. It's personalized to your actual income, not a generic list."

[SCREEN: change the paycheck number, show the queue visibly re-order; a previously-skipped bonus becomes available]

**Savings + credit-card sequencers (faster):**
> "Same idea for savings — you tell it how much capital you can move, and it sequences the
> highest-ROI savings bonuses one at a time, around the hold periods. And for credit cards, you
> give it your monthly spending and it ranks cards by the bonus you'd actually clear, factoring
> the annual fee and your everyday earn rate — and it's 5/24-aware, so it tells you when your
> next Chase slot opens and which categories your wallet isn't covering yet."

[SCREEN: quick cuts — savings sequencer ROI list; credit-card sequencer with a 5/24 countdown + "category gap: groceries" callout]

## PART 4 — THE POWER-USER PLAYS (1-2 to flex, pick your favorite) (~40s)

> "Two more for the power users."

**0% APR arbitrage:**
> "This one's my favorite play. Some cards give you 0% interest for a year and a half, two years.

> in a 4-and-a-half-percent savings account, and let the bank float your purchases for free. Stacks
> OS models the whole thing: how much interest you earn while the bank carries the balance, plus
> the rewards, plus the sign-up bonus — net of tax. It turns '0% APR' into an actual number."

[SCREEN: 0% APR calculator — pick a card, show the month-by-month float interest curve + total return]

**Debt simulator (if relevant to the video):**

[SCREEN: debt simulator — debts in, payoff timeline + "interest saved" out]
